# Resume Feedback — Shreyas

Straight up: this is a strong resume. Two years of BSL2 cancer/microbiology research with *quantified results* (~70% growth reduction at 128 µg/ml, checkerboard assays), hours tracked for every commitment, clean layout with real section structure. The content does not need to grow — it needs to be **edited down**. Your problem is the opposite of most people's: you say everything twice and bury your best numbers mid-paragraph. Work through this list in order.

This is [CURRICULUM.md](../lessons/CURRICULUM.md) applied to a document — review it like a repo. Your agent can help find problems; every word of the fix is yours.

## 1. Fix today — privacy 🔒

Your full street address and phone number are in this resume, and this resume is in a **public GitHub repo**.

- Replace the street address with "League City, TX" — city and state is all a resume needs.
- Decide deliberately whether the phone number stays in the public copy (a version with just email + city is safer for the repo; the full-contact version goes to actual applications).
- Bonus housekeeping: there's a Word lock file (`~$reyas_Bishnu_Resume.docx`) sitting in this folder. It's not committed, but add `~$*` to `.gitignore` so it never is.

## 2. The objective is welded to one application

It names the UTMB Junior Volunteer Summer Program specifically. Perfect *for that application* — wrong for every other reader who finds this resume. Keep a master version with one neutral sentence (or no objective at all), and tailor a copy per application. The master lives in the repo; the tailored ones go out the door.

## 3. Make the dates agree with each other

The objective says "sophomore," the GPA says "(9th Grade)," the school says "2025 – Present," and both 9th- and 10th-grade coursework are listed. Each line is probably true *as of some date* — but they're different dates. Pick one as-of date (today) and make every line consistent with it.

## 4. Say it once (Skill #2 — have I seen this before?)

- **STEM Siblings Club appears three times** — volunteer list, leadership section, extracurriculars — with the same 60+ hours each time. Pick its strongest home (leadership), keep one line elsewhere at most, and let the hours appear once.
- The volunteer section lists every organization in a table *and then* repeats them as descriptive bullets. Pick one format.
- **Boy Scouts Patrol Leader has seven bullets.** That's padding — the best three say everything: responsibility for the patrol, leading PLC-level planning, mentoring younger scouts through rank advancement. Cut the rest.

## 5. Free your research numbers from the walls of text

Each study is one ~8-line paragraph with the impressive result buried in the middle. Convert each to 3–4 bullets, **result first**:

> - Found Ivacaftor–Pentamidine combination reduced drug-resistant *Salmonella* growth by >75% (checkerboard assay)
> - Determined MICs across dose ranges for two FDA-approved repurposing candidates
> - 2nd place, Biology & Microbiology, district science fair; qualified for Science & Engineering Fair of Houston

Also: "Presented findings at the school followed with district science fair" appears in both studies — same grammar slip, copy-pasted. It should read "…at the school *and then the* district science fair." (Copy-pasted sentences are how one mistake becomes two.)

## 6. Small fixes

- Honors aren't in reverse-chronological order (2023 fencing sits below 2026 science fair; 4th place 2025 above 2nd place 2026). Newest first, strongest first.
- "Worlds VEX Robotics Qualifier" → "VEX Robotics Worlds Qualifier."
- Page 3 contains exactly two lines. After the dedup pass above, this resume fits on **one page** — and a one-page version of this content is genuinely impressive.

## 7. What's missing

A link to your website and GitHub in the header. You're building a portfolio site with a real commit history — that's evidence, not decoration.
