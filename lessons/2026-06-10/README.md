# Lessons

Your fundamentals are the strongest of the group: a real build step, ES6 modules, consistent BEM, small commits, and — best of all — two reverts in your history, which means you experiment and walk things back. That's how real engineering works.

So your lessons aren't about mechanics. They're about **judgment**: knowing what to delete, what to harden, and what actually matters to users versus what just looks impressive.

Work through them in order:

1. [The CSS audit — delete dead code](01-css-audit.md)
2. [Make the contact form real](02-real-form-handling.md)
3. [Performance pass and first tests](03-performance-and-tests.md)

Rules of engagement:

- You can use the LLM to help, but for each lesson, *you* write the plan first and explain it before generating any code.
- Keep doing what you're doing with commits — small, specific, honest.
- Lesson 1 must produce **no visual change**. Lessons 2 and 3 change behavior on purpose.
